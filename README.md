Photovoltaic panel defect detection remains challenging due to the presence of tiny targets, low-contrast anomalies, elongated crack patterns, irregular defect boundaries, and strong background texture interference. Existing one-stage detectors often suffer from insufficient global context modeling and weak local morphology representation, leading to missed detections and inaccurate localization of subtle defects. To address these issues, we propose \textbf{FCD-YOLO}, an improved YOLOv12-based detector built upon a \emph{Frequency-Context Dual Enhancement} paradigm for photovoltaic defect detection. Specifically, we design a \textbf{Wavelet-guided Hybrid Cross-Attention Mamba (WHCA-Mamba)} module to strengthen context-aware feature modeling. The module first performs complementary interaction between split feature branches via cross-attention, and then introduces wavelet decomposition to explicitly separate low-frequency structural information from high-frequency detail components. The high-frequency branch is further enhanced by Mamba state-space modeling, enabling efficient long-range dependency learning and more stable responses to weak anomalies and slender cracks. To further compensate for the limitations of global aggregation in capturing local defect morphology, we propose a \textbf{Frequency-Direction Adaptive Module (FDAM)}. FDAM explicitly enhances defect-sensitive representations through three complementary branches, namely frequency-aware detail enhancement, direction-aware structural encoding, and deformation-aware geometric adaptation, followed by dynamic fusion and lightweight channel attention to adaptively emphasize discriminative defect cues while suppressing background interference. By deploying WHCA-Mamba in the shallow-to-middle stages of the backbone and introducing FDAM for subsequent local feature refinement, FCD-YOLO jointly improves global semantic understanding and local morphology discrimination. Extensive experiments on multiple public photovoltaic defect datasets demonstrate that FCD-YOLO consistently outperforms strong YOLO baselines and representative state-of-the-art methods, achieving superior robustness for tiny, low-contrast, and structurally complex defects with favorable computational efficiency.
